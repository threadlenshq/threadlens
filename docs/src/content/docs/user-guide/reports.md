Reports preserve the conclusions from useful scout runs and selected findings. They depend on AI provider configuration and enough selected findings to summarize.

:::caution[Reports need AI and signal]
If no AI provider path is configured, report generation cannot produce useful analysis. If the selected findings are unrelated or low-signal, refine the query and run another scout before generating a report.
:::

## Research reports

Research reports cluster social posts into pain themes, extract supporting quotes, and suggest product angles. Generate them after selecting findings that represent a coherent market question.

Good inputs for a research report include repeated complaints, workarounds, buying triggers, or audience-specific language from multiple findings.

## Google reports

Google reports summarize search results, opportunities, risks, and next actions. Use them to understand search intent and external pages around a market.

Google reports also depend on the configured search provider and AI provider readiness.

## Report hygiene

- Name projects clearly before generating reports.
- Exclude low-signal findings before report generation.
- Select findings that answer the same research question.
- Treat reports as research summaries, not final product strategy.

For first-run setup, see [Configuration Basics](../start-here/configuration-basics/). For the first report decision point, see [Create Your First Project](../start-here/first-project/).
